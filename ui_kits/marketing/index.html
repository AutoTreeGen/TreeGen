The thesis

Genealogy is not a hobby. It is the guesswork evidence we owe to the people who came before us — and the ones who will come after.

No. 01

Cite, or it didn't happen.

Every fact in your tree must trace back to a source — a parish register, a census line, a centiMorgan segment. Unsupported claims are stored separately, as hypotheses.

QUAY 1 · 2 · 3
No. 02

Disagreements are versioned, never overwritten.

Two cousins, two birth years, two scribes' handwriting. The archive remembers all of them. Confidence is computed, not asserted, and updates with new evidence.

audit trail · permanent
No. 03

Paper and DNA, in one workspace.

Y-DNA, mtDNA, autosomal cM segments and your scanned vital records sit in the same tree. The inference engine reads both at once.

Methodology

From GEDCOM and DNA
to a defensible tree.

i

Import

Drop a GEDCOM 5.5.5 file or connect FamilySearch. We parse strictly and tell you which records bend the spec.

≤ 60 sec · any size
ii

Match

Upload Y, mt and autosomal kits or sync from GEDmatch / 23andMe / Ancestry. Each kit becomes its own evidence stream.

cM · segments · haplogroups
iii

Hypothesise

The inference engine proposes parent–child links, scores them, and records the evidence both for and against.

confidence ∈ [0, 1]
iv

Cite

Lock a finding into the canonical tree only with at least one QUAY-graded source. Disagreements are versioned, never overwritten.

QUAY 2+ to commit
